# 00 — Vision

## The feeling

Mesmerizing. Magical. Futuristic. Epic. Within the first few seconds, a visitor should
feel they've stepped into something from the future — and that SARAB is the company that
lives there.

This is not a website that *describes* SARAB. It's a website that makes you *feel* what
SARAB does: takes the ordinary and makes it extraordinary, instantly. The site itself is a
demonstration of that promise.

## The one goal

Turn a first-time visitor into a booking. Every scene, animation, and word serves that.
When someone finishes the scroll, the thought we want is: *"I need this at my event — how
do I book?"* — and the answer is right there (WhatsApp, or leave your details).

## Who's visiting

People planning events in the region — couples planning weddings, companies planning
launches and conferences, event planners and agencies, venue owners. Mostly non-technical:
they don't care how the AI works, they care how it makes their event look and feel. Some
browse in Arabic, some Hebrew, some English. Many are on their phones.

## The emotional arc of the scroll

Treat the page as a short film, not a stack of sections:

1. **The hook** — an opening that stops the scroll. Something alive, in motion, that says
   "future" before a single word is read.
2. **The promise** — who SARAB is, in one confident line. The first-of-its-kind-in-the-region
   claim as a flex.
3. **The proof** — the before/after transformations, revealed cinematically. This is the
   heart. This is what makes people book.
4. **The energy** — the reels, woven in as part of the world, showing the experience live
   at real events.
5. **The invitation** — the booking moment, made to feel effortless and inevitable.

## The wow levers

- The **before/after reveal** is the single most persuasive thing on the site. Treat it as
  the climax.
- **First of its kind in the region** — lean into it. It's a genuine differentiator.
- **No photographer, studio-quality, in seconds** — the magic is the speed and the result.
- The **craft of the site itself** is proof of taste. If the website is this good, the
  product must be.

## What success looks like

Someone sends the link to a friend with "look at this." Someone screenshots a
before/after. Someone taps WhatsApp before they even finish scrolling.
