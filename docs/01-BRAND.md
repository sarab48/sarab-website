# 01 — Brand

## Who SARAB is

SARAB is an AI solutions company. Its flagship is an **AI photobooth** that transforms
ordinary event photos into studio-quality images in seconds — no photographer, no studio,
no waiting. It is the **first of its kind in the region**, and that pride is part of the
identity.

SARAB is built from genuine excitement about what AI can do — not hype, not fear. That
spirit should come through: confident, forward-looking, a little bit magic.

## The offer, in plain terms

- Ordinary event photo in → studio-quality, transformed image out
- In seconds, on-site, at the event
- No photographer needed
- Guests get something they've never seen before and want to share

## Positioning & differentiators

- **First of its kind in the region** — the headline flex.
- **Speed + quality together** — studio results without the studio or the wait.
- **An experience, not a service** — part of the event's wow, not a vendor in the corner.
- **AI-native** — SARAB isn't a photo company dabbling in AI; AI is the whole point.

## Voice & tone

Premium, confident, warm, a touch cinematic. Speak to the feeling, not the technology.
Short, bold lines beat long paragraphs. Avoid jargon — the audience is non-technical.

Across the three languages:

- **Arabic** — colloquial Levantine warmth where it fits; this is the heart language for
  much of the audience. Authentic, not stiff MSA marketing-speak — unless a moment calls
  for elevated tone.
- **English** — polished, premium, international.
- **Hebrew** — natural and current, matching the local market.

The owner has strong editorial instincts and writes/speaks Levantine Arabic natively.
**Draft copy in all three languages, but flag every piece for the owner's review** —
especially the Arabic, which they're best placed to perfect. Never ship copy as final
without their sign-off.

## Logo & identity

The logo and roll-up poster live in `public/assets/`. Treat the logo as a sacred identity
element — clear space, never distorted, used at high-impact moments (the opening, the
close). Extract its palette during asset analysis and evaluate whether it can drive the
site's colors (see `docs/02-DESIGN.md` and the owner's direction: use the logo colors if
they serve the creative vision; evolve from them if not).

## Words to lean on

Transform, magic, future, instant, studio-quality, first in the region, experience,
unforgettable. (Translate the *feeling*, not the literal words, into Arabic and Hebrew.)
